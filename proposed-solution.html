Team's Problem: Micro-mobility Device Theft on Campus

Micro-mobility device theft is a common issue at UC San Diego, where limited tracking and monitoring make it difficult to prevent or resolve stolen e-scooters and bikes.

Our proposed solution will combine the advantages of the existing solutions mentioned above. It will have a GPS tag to detect location, a gyroscope/accelerometer to detect motion, an alarm to notify nearby people that the device is stolen, and a camera + motor to capture the thief's faces. We plan to use the ESP32 chip, which has WiFi included, so that the owner of the device can communicate with their device via network protocol using a web interface.

Problem Motivation

  1. We chose this issue because many students rely on personal micro-mobility devices for their daily commutes across campus. Nobody wants to walk uphill both ways with a midterm in 10 minutes :(
  2. The growing number of thefts has created financial stress, safety concerns, and student transportation disruptions. It's a problem affecting the quality of life and trust in campus safety services.
  3. Maybe the owner has special connection to their micro-mobility devices. We want to prevent people from losing their beloving ones.

Current Existing Solutions

GPS Tags and Trackers
  • How It Works:
    Devices are attached to scooters or bikes and paired with a mobile app. If movement is detected without authorization, they can trigger alarms or send location updates.
  • Strengths:
    Real-time location tracking and potential recovery of stolen items.
  • Limitations:
    Can be removed or disabled by thieves.
    Often dependent on battery life or cellular signal.
    Doesn't prevent theft, only helps after the fact.
U-locks and Heavy-duty Chains
  • How It Works:
    Mechanical locks secure devices to bike racks or fixed objects.
  • Strengths:
    It's easy to use and widely available.
  • Limitations:
    Can still be cut with tools.
    Do not alert users if it's being stolen.

Our Proposed Solution

Block Diagram and 3D view
What the Technology Will Do
  • Detect if the micro-mobility device is moved or stolen using motion sensors
  • Trigger an audible alarm in real-time when theft is detected
  • Track the live GPS location of the stolen device
  • Capture and upload a photo of the thief using a built-in camera
  • Allow owners to monitor and control the system through a web interface
Technological Aspects of Solution
  • ESP32 microcontroller with built-in WiFi for communication
  • Accelerometer and gyroscope module for motion detection
  • GPS module for real-time tracking
  • Camera module (ESP32-CAM) for facial image capture
  • Web server hosted on the ESP32 for real-time monitoring
  • Alarm module to alert nearby people
